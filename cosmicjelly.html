cosmic jellyfish

Hello! I don’t have a scientific name, because I’m that new to the world, but you can call me, Cosmic Jellyfish.

I live 3000 metres deep in the ocean.

I can eat ten times my body weight in one day, my favourite bites are small animals.

I’m afraid of my own existence.

Appearance
Like most jellyfish, this majestic creature is a brainless blob, that floats around propelled by its many long, thin tentacles.

Its organs glow in the dark, with bright vibrant colours, giving it its name. The jellyfishes’ body is a soft jelly-like, housing all its organs. It has 6 yellow pockets on its side, thought to be for reproductive organs.

In the bottom middle of the animal there is a red organ, thought to be for eating and digestion.
Relation to humans
What sets this species apart from the rest is just how little is known about it. A total of two recordings exist of the animal and almost all information on it is purely speculative.

No attempt has been made to capture this animal and find out more about it, as its population is thought to be in the single digits.
