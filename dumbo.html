Dumbo Octopus

Hi! My name is Grimpoteuthis, but people often refer to me as Dumbo Octopus, because I remind them of the baby elephant named Dumbo in a 1941 Disney film.

I live 1000 to 7000 metres deep in the ocean.

I love eating small worms, planktons and shellfish.

I’m so scared of sharks, that I most of the time camouflage myself to blend into the appearance of the ocean floor.

Appearance
Dumbo octopus is known for its prominent ear-like fin which extends above its eyes. This animal is capable of changing its color from pink to red, brown or white. They commonly weigh one kilogram and their length is around 30 centimeters. Their average lifespan is between three to five years. It hasn’t been proven that the Dumbo octopus produces ink for jet-propulsion, which makes sense considering its habitat. It swims by using the movement of its fins. Another distinct characteristic Dumbo octopuses have are their white spots near above their eyes, which detect light.
Relation to humans
Humans aren’t considered as a threat to Dumbo octopuses, because they live that deep in the ocean. Their population is quite healthy and they aren’t considered endangered.
